# Diriqo User Guide

This guide is a practical help center for everyday work in Diriqo.
It covers the current system: administration, customers, jobs, calculations, offers, billing, workers, communication and the customer portal.

## 1. Main Menu

- `Dashboard` = daily overview with jobs, alerts, finances, leads, offers, absences and worked hours.
- `Jobs` = all field work, dates, work status, billing status, photos, shifts, workers and communication.
- `Customers` = companies and people you work for, including contacts, billing details, calculations, offers and jobs.
- `Billing` = internal invoices, PDF downloads, QR payments, invoice statuses and Pohoda export.
- `Workers` = employees, monthly shift overview, rewards, advances and absences.
- `Calendar` = planning events and dates.
- `Absences` = holidays, sickness and other time off.
- `Advance requests` = worker advance payment requests.
- `Leads` = inquiries from the website and the customer portal.
- `Calculations` = internal pricing, costs, margin and calculation versions.
- `Offers` = customer-facing offers, online link, PDF, e-mail and conversion to a job.
- `My account` = signed-in account, active company, supplier billing data, bank data and password change.
- `Help` = this help center.

## 2. Recommended Workflow

1. Create or find the `customer`.
2. Add the `contact person` and customer billing details.
3. Create a `calculation` or continue from an inquiry.
4. Create an `offer` from the calculation.
5. Send or copy the online offer link to the customer.
6. After approval, create a `job from the offer`.
7. Add workers, shifts, photo documentation and communication.
8. Mark the job as done when work is completed.
9. Create an `invoice` from completed uninvoiced jobs.
10. Issue the invoice, download PDF or QR payment, and export to Pohoda if needed.

## 3. Dashboard and Operations Check

The dashboard shows what needs attention right now.

- Use the top quick links for new jobs, calendar, customers and workers.
- Switch between today's and tomorrow's jobs.
- Check ordered, active, completed, ready-to-invoice, invoiced and paid jobs.
- Review monthly economy: ordered value, ready-to-invoice value, invoiced value, labor costs, other costs, profit and advances.
- Watch alerts for leads, overdue offers, advance requests, absences and today's worked hours.

Practical routine:

1. Open `Dashboard` in the morning.
2. Check today's and tomorrow's jobs.
3. Review alerts: leads, offers, advances and absences.
4. Check monthly economy and jobs ready for billing.

## 4. Creating a Customer

1. Open `Customers`.
2. Click `+ New customer`.
3. Fill in at least the customer name.
4. Add e-mail and phone if available.
5. If you know the company number, use `Load from ARES`.
6. Check billing name, VAT number, street, city, postal code and country.
7. Save the customer.
8. The customer detail opens after saving.

Why it matters:

- The customer card is the base record.
- Contacts, leads, calculations, offers, jobs and invoices are connected to it.
- Billing details are used later when creating invoices.

## 5. Customer Detail

The customer detail contains all connected work.

- `Billing details` prepare the customer for invoicing.
- `Calculations` show internal price calculations.
- `Offers` show offers prepared for the customer.
- `Contacts` are people selected on jobs, offers and e-mails.
- `Customer jobs` show job history and current states.

When updating billing details:

1. Open the customer.
2. Open customer editing.
3. Fill company number, VAT number, billing name, address and contacts.
4. Save changes.

## 6. Adding a Contact Person

1. Open `Customers`.
2. Click the customer.
3. In `Contacts`, click `+ Add contact`.
4. Fill in the name.
5. Ideally add role, phone, e-mail and note.
6. Save the contact.

The contact can be used on jobs, offers, e-mails and portal access.

## 7. Leads

Leads come from the website or customer portal.

- The `Leads` list shows contact, company, message, service, source and status.
- Open a lead by clicking it.
- Create or find the related customer.
- Continue with a calculation, offer or job.
- Delete irrelevant leads if needed.

Recommended flow:

1. Open `Leads`.
2. Check new items.
3. Verify whether the customer already exists.
4. Add the contact to the customer.
5. Continue with a calculation or offer.

## 8. Calculations

A calculation is the internal base for pricing and costs.

### Option A: Calculation for a Customer

1. Open `Customers`.
2. Click the customer.
3. In `Calculations`, click `+ New calculation`.

### Option B: Calculation from the Main Menu

1. Open `Calculations`.
2. Click `+ New calculation`.
3. Select a customer or create a calculation without one if the customer is not clear yet.

In a calculation, fill in:

- Calculation title, date, status, description and internal note.
- Customer items that should be copied to the offer.
- Internal cost items visible only to the company.
- Quantity, unit, unit price, VAT rate and notes.

The system calculates:

- Total customer price.
- Total costs.
- Expected profit.
- Labor costs.
- Other costs.

## 9. Calculation Versions and Offer Creation

Use the calculation as internal working material.

- Edit the calculation when pricing changes.
- Use version history where available to see previous calculation states.
- Create the customer offer with `Create offer`.

When an offer is created:

- Customer-facing items are copied into the offer.
- Internal costs are not copied as public customer items.
- The offer can still be edited before sending.

## 10. Offers

`Offers` are the customer-facing proposals.

On offer detail you can:

- Edit title, date, validity, status, discount, contact person and texts.
- Open the online offer.
- Copy the online offer link.
- Download PDF where available.
- Refresh pricing from the source calculation.
- Send an updated offer by e-mail.
- Mark the offer as not interested.
- Delete the offer.

Check offer states regularly:

- Follow up expired offers.
- Convert an approved offer to a job.
- Mark rejected offers as `Not interested`.

## 11. Creating a Job from an Offer

1. Open the offer detail.
2. Click `Create job from offer`.
3. Fill in the start date.
4. Fill in the end date.
5. Add the job address.
6. Add the job description.
7. Click `Create job and prepare e-mail`.

The system:

- Creates a job for the same customer.
- Marks the offer as accepted.
- Sends or prepares a schedule confirmation e-mail when a contact e-mail is available.

Important:

- A contact e-mail is needed for smooth confirmation.
- After creation, check the job, workers and dates.

## 12. Jobs

A job is the actual work to be done.

Jobs can be created:

- Manually from `Jobs` and `+ New job`.
- From an offer.
- As a repeated or multi-day job when configured.

Watch on every job:

- Customer and main contact.
- Title, description and address.
- Start and end time.
- Price.
- Work status.
- Billing status.
- Workers and shifts.
- Photo documentation.
- Communication.

## 13. Work Days, Shifts and Workers

The job detail contains workers and shifts.

- Add workers ideally while creating the job.
- Shifts show actual working time.
- Multi-day jobs track individual working days.
- Hours and rates feed job economy and worker payroll overview.

Recommended flow:

1. Open the job.
2. Check assigned workers.
3. Add or correct shifts.
4. Verify worked hours.
5. Check job economy.

## 14. Job Economy

Job economy shows whether the work is profitable.

The system calculates:

- Job revenue.
- Labor costs from shifts and rates.
- Other costs.
- Expected profit.
- Total hours.

Practical notes:

- Job price is revenue.
- Worker shifts and rates are labor costs.
- Add other costs where they are not already in the calculation.
- Check economy before billing the job.

## 15. Job Photo Documentation

Jobs can contain photos.

- Photos are split into `Before` and `After`.
- Open photo documentation in the job detail.
- Click a photo to preview it.
- Download the file when available.

Use photos for:

- Documenting the initial state.
- Documenting finished work.
- Customer communication.
- Claims and quality checks.

## 16. Communication

Diriqo can record e-mail communication.

- Job detail includes a communication section.
- Offers can be sent as updated e-mails.
- Replies and messages are stored in the communication feed when they pass through the e-mail integration.
- Messages are linked to the job, offer or related entity by thread.

Recommendation:

- Send important schedule confirmations through the system.
- Use online offer links or the send button for offers.
- Keep job communication as the agreement history.

## 17. Workers

`Workers` shows people in the company and their monthly overview.

The list shows:

- Active workers.
- Current absence state.
- Shifts for the selected month.
- Work logs.
- Assigned jobs.
- Advances and payroll calculation.

Month filter:

- Switch between previous, current and next payroll month.
- The work month follows the calendar month.
- Payroll advances use a separate period from day 19 to day 17 of the next month.
- Payroll date is connected to day 18 of the next month.

## 18. Worker Detail

The worker detail contains:

- Summary stats.
- Jobs and assignments.
- Daily job breakdown.
- Shifts.
- Advances and finance settings.

To check payroll:

1. Open `Workers`.
2. Select the correct month.
3. Open the worker.
4. Check shifts, hours, rate and advances.
5. Correct shifts or finance settings if needed.

## 19. Absences

`Absences` are used for holidays, sickness and other time off.

- Absences have type, status, start and end.
- Approved absences appear in worker overview and dashboard.
- Check absences before planning workers on jobs.

Recommended flow:

1. Open `Absences`.
2. Check new or active absences.
3. Approve or update the status.
4. Consider absences when planning jobs.

## 20. Advance Requests

`Advance requests` show money requests from workers before payroll.

- A request has worker, amount, reason, status and date.
- Approved or paid advances appear in monthly worker overview.
- Dashboard highlights requests waiting for action.

Practical flow:

1. Open `Advance requests`.
2. Check new requests.
3. Approve, reject or mark as paid.
4. Check the effect on the worker detail.

## 21. Calendar

Calendar is used for time planning.

- Calendar shows events by day.
- Create a new event with `+ New event`.
- Open event detail by clicking it.
- Use events for internal organization, date checks and non-job work.

## 22. Billing

Billing turns completed jobs into documents.

Create a new invoice:

1. Open `Billing`.
2. Click `Create invoice`.
3. Select the customer.
4. Click `Show jobs`.
5. Select completed uninvoiced jobs with a price.
6. Check issue date, taxable supply date, due date and VAT rate.
7. Add a note if needed.
8. Click `Create invoice`.

Important:

- Usually only completed jobs should be invoiced.
- Jobs without a price are normally not offered for invoicing.
- If your company has no VAT number, the invoice is created as non-VAT payer with 0% VAT.
- Supplier and customer snapshots are saved so the invoice remains stable after later contact edits.

## 23. Invoice Detail

On invoice detail you can:

- Edit invoice draft.
- Load supplier data from `My account`.
- Issue the invoice.
- Mark it as sent.
- Mark it as paid.
- Cancel the invoice.
- Permanently delete a draft or non-exported invoice.
- Download PDF.
- View or download QR payment.
- Export to Pohoda.
- Download the last XML export.

Invoice states:

- `Draft` = still editable.
- `Issued` = has invoice number and is ready to send.
- `Sent` = sent to the customer.
- `Paid` = payment is done.
- `Overdue` = follow-up is needed.
- `Cancelled` = should no longer be used.

## 24. Pohoda Export

Pohoda export creates an XML file for selected invoices.

Steps:

1. Open `Billing`.
2. Filter invoices by status or export state.
3. Tick invoices for export.
4. Click `Export to Pohoda`.
5. The system creates XML and marks invoices as exported.
6. XML downloads immediately and is also available from invoice detail.

Rules:

- Only issued, sent, paid or overdue invoices can be exported.
- Drafts without invoice number cannot be exported.
- Exported invoices should not be deleted to keep accounting history.

## 25. My Account and Company Billing Data

`My account` contains:

- Signed-in e-mail.
- Profile and role.
- Active company.
- Company memberships.
- Billing data for the active company.
- Bank data for invoices and QR payments.
- Password change.

Before first billing, check:

1. Company name.
2. Company number and VAT number.
3. Billing address.
4. Bank account and bank code.
5. IBAN and SWIFT/BIC if needed.
6. Save billing data.

## 26. Customer Portal

The customer portal is a separate customer-facing area.

Customers can see:

- Dashboard.
- Their jobs.
- Their inquiries.
- Offers.
- Billing.
- Password change and logout.

The portal only shows customer-safe data for the specific customer and contact.

## 27. Portal Offers, Inquiries and Invoices

In the portal, the customer can:

- View offers.
- Approve an offer while it is still open.
- Send offer responses: interested, contact request, revision request or not interested.
- Create a new inquiry.
- Track their jobs and billing.

For administration:

- Portal inquiries appear in `Leads`.
- Portal offer approval is stored on the offer.
- Customer responses can be used for the next sales step.

## 28. Languages

The system supports multiple languages.

- Use the language switcher where available.
- Internal entered data stays exactly as users write it.
- Translations affect navigation, labels and interface text.

## 29. Common Scenarios

### New company from website or phone

1. Check `Leads`.
2. Create or find the `customer`.
3. Add `contact`.
4. Create a `calculation`.
5. Create an `offer` from the calculation.

### Customer approved an offer

1. Open `Offers`.
2. Open the offer.
3. Click `Create job from offer`.
4. Fill date, address and description.
5. Check the new job.

### Job is done and should be invoiced

1. Open the job.
2. Check work status, price, shifts and economy.
3. Mark the job as done if needed.
4. Open `Billing`.
5. Create invoice from completed uninvoiced jobs.
6. Issue invoice and download PDF.

### Quick payroll check

1. Open `Workers`.
2. Select the correct month.
3. Check total hours and advances.
4. Open worker detail.
5. Check shifts, rate and finances.

### Customer needs portal access

1. Open the customer.
2. Check contact person and e-mail.
3. Create or verify portal user using the available admin action.
4. Customer signs in at `/portal/login`.

## 30. Short Advice

- Always check whether the customer already exists before creating a new one.
- Add customer contact and billing details early.
- Use calculation as the internal pricing truth.
- Use offer as the customer-facing document.
- Create job only when the work is agreed.
- Add workers and shifts continuously, not only at month end.
- Store `Before` and `After` photos directly on the job.
- Invoice only completed and price-checked jobs.
- Before Pohoda export, verify invoice number, supplier, customer and totals.
- Company billing data in `My account` is important for PDF, QR payment and export.

## 31. One Sentence Summary

`Customer -> contact -> calculation -> offer -> job -> shifts and photos -> done -> invoice -> PDF or Pohoda`
